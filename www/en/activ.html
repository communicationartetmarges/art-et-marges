OPENING EXPO CA$H

THURDAY 16 MAY, 2024, 6-8.30PM

How do art brut artists talk about money? Is money present in their work, and in what forms?

Answers can be found in the CA$H exhibition, curated by Éric Gauthier of Galerie du moineau écarlate (Paris) and Céline Delavaux, in collaboration with the Art et marges museum.

In situ creation by visual artist Noé Peyre during the opening.

Free opening without reservation.

CA$H WORKSHOPS AT STERPUT

SUNDAY MAY 26, SATURDAY JUNE 22 2:00-4:00 PM

SUNDAY SEPTEMBER 8 2:00-6:00 PM

In collaboration with the Art et marges museum, Sterput is hosting a series of workshops open to all in connection with the CA$H exhibition. Led by Naïla Akli and Vincent Wagnair, the aim of these creative workshops is to appropriate and manufacture fake banknotes in all techniques. All materials will be made available to participants.

The " fake banknotes " made at Sterput during the workshops and those produced by the public of the Art et marges museum during their visit, will be brought together to be presented at an exhibition which will take place at Sterput from Thursday September 19, 2024 6pm to Sunday September 29, 2024 5pm.

Participants can get their banknotes Sunday September 29, 2024 from 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.

Workshop open to all | open price (suggested price: 8€) | booking: info@sterput.org

Le Sterput: Place du Jeu de Balle, 68 - 1000 Brussels (a stone's throw from the Art et marges museum)

FILM + MEETING AT NOVA | CASH - CUT TO THE QUICK

ÉRIC GAUTHIER, 2024, FR, DCP, FR, 52’

FRIDAY, MAY 31 8:00 PM

Éric Gauthier runs the Galerie du Moineau écarlate in Paris, which brings together spontaneous, off-the-wall creations, uneducated voices, the fruit of visionary destinies... He also makes short videos in which he interviews so-called "brut" artists about their relationship with money. "Cash - Cut to the Quick" is the filmic result of this approach. In front of his camera, a dozen French and Belgian artists talk, in cash terms, about their work and the material conditions of their creation. These interviews are accompanied by comments from Céline Delavaux, author of "L'Art brut, un fantasme de peintre".

Screening followed by a discussion with Éric Gauthier and Céline Delavaux.

At Cinéma Nova: rue d'Arenberg 3, 1000 Brussels | €4
